Input to System

What the customer sends, and what Northwoods builds

Customer Sends

  • Current service list and rough prices
  • Screenshot examples of customer texts
  • Photos of past work or job types
  • Current quote/invoice habits
  • Common questions customers ask
  • Examples of lost leads or messy follow-ups
  • Preferred contact method and service area

  • Google request form questions

  • Quote sheet and pricing fields
  • Job packet checklist
  • Photo/file naming system
  • Follow-up templates and weekly review checklist
Deliverable Stack

What the finished package includes

This is the “bigger” offer that can be sold after a smaller cleanup snapshot proves the customer needs a system.

1. Landing Page

Headline, service cards, process section, request button, and customer-ready wording.

2. Intake Form

Service type, photos, measurements, budget, timing, address/service area, and desired outcome.

3. Lead + Job Tracker

Status, priority, price, follow-up date, next action, notes, files, and job stage.

4. Quote Sheet

Materials, labor, trip/setup, markup, notes, optional add-ons, and customer total.

5. Job Packet

Customer info, job notes, photos needed, checklist, risk notes, and completion notes.

6. Follow-Up Pack

Missing-info, quote sent, reminder, unpaid invoice, schedule change, and review request templates.

Build Plan

How the intensive example could be delivered

A full build should still stay controlled. The best version is phased.

Phase 1

Cleanup Snapshot

Sort the messy details, define service buckets, identify missing info, and decide whether a full system is worth building.

Phase 2

Form + Tracker

Build the intake form, lead/job tracker, statuses, priority levels, next-action column, and file link structure.

Phase 3

Quote + Templates

Build quote sheet, pricing fields, material/labor sections, missing-info messages, quote follow-up, and invoice reminder templates.

Phase 4

Landing Page + Launch

Create the service page, connect the request button, add boundaries, check mobile layout, and give the owner a weekly review checklist.
