Clear terms

Know what happens next.

What is the best way to start?

Send the photos, notes, measurements, screenshots, files, constraints, and desired result you already have. Highway 38 identifies the smallest useful service from the approved catalog.

Does submitting a request create a charge?

No. You approve scope, price, payment terms, turnaround, included work, exclusions, assumptions, and revision allowance before work begins.

What do I receive?

The exact deliverable depends on the approved service and may include a plan, annotated image, workflow map, checklist, quote, decision packet, configured workflow, documentation, or business-system implementation.

How long does work take?

Published services include a customer-readable turnaround in business days. Larger systems receive a written schedule after requirements and acceptance criteria are confirmed.

What counts as a revision?

A revision adjusts the approved deliverable using the original goal and source information. A new space, output, machine, workflow, integration, or materially changed requirement is a scope change.

Can Highway 38 verify measurements?

No. Customer measurements are treated as supplied or assumed. Critical dimensions and field conditions must be verified before purchasing, fabrication, permitting, or construction.

Are examples customer projects?

Only items explicitly labeled verified proof should be treated as customer proof. Representative and hypothetical examples explain workflow and output style without claiming a real customer result.

Does Highway 38 provide engineering, permit, legal, accounting, or tax approval?

No. Highway 38 provides planning, organization, workflow, and decision support. Licensed or regulated work remains with the appropriate qualified professional.

Can the system send, post, pay, or file automatically?

Important external actions remain approval-controlled unless an owner explicitly authorizes a separately reviewed workflow. The Business Office does not automatically move money, fund payroll, file returns, share customer proof, or publish social content.

How are private records handled?

Submit only information needed for the approved scope. Credentials, tokens, private keys, payment credentials, and unnecessary sensitive information should not be placed in public forms.

What happens if the project changes?

The affected work pauses, the change is documented, and revised scope and pricing are prepared. Added work is never assumed automatically.

What if the request is outside Highway 38’s scope?

The request is narrowed, declined, or referred to a qualified professional. Highway 38 does not imply authority it does not have.
