## What Was Kept From Old Directions

### From ForgeIQ

Kept:

- The productized cleanup concept.
- The idea of turning rough customer inputs into finished outputs.
- The 36-product ladder concept.
- The “organized first” strategy.
- The practical outputs: plans, trackers, quote sheets, dashboards, forms, packets, and simple systems.

Retired:

- ForgeIQ as a public brand.
- ForgeIQ as the homepage/company name.
- Tech/app-heavy positioning.

### From Northwoods Problem Solvers

Kept:

- The idea of service desks/categories.
- Practical local problem-solving tone.
- Project, business, digital, shop, and cleanup lanes.

Retired:

- Northwoods Problem Solvers as the main brand.
- Northwoods Workbench as the system name.

### From Industrial Logic Solutions

Kept:

- Practical shop/workflow/planning credibility.
- CNC, automation, quoting, workflow, and process-improvement experience as background strength.

Retired:

- Industrial Logic Solutions as a customer-facing name.

### From GarageOS / WrenchIQ

Kept:

- Simple operating-system idea for garages, shops, and local service businesses.
- Automotive/shop workflow planning ideas.
- Customer/job tracker, quote sheet, and service workflow concepts.

Retired:

- GarageOS and WrenchIQ as public brand names.

### From RangeRivet Works

Kept:

- Durable, practical, shop-capable tone.
- Long-term compatibility with a future repair/welding/shop branch.

Retired:

- RangeRivet Works as the main name.

## Archive Rule

Old files, scripts, docs, commit history, or repo paths can keep legacy names if changing them would break tooling or create unnecessary cleanup work.

Public-facing pages, README, current docs, website navigation, hero copy, product/package descriptions, and customer-facing offers should use **Highway 38 Solutions** only.
