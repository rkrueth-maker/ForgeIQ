What This Page Proves

Every project has a clear input, output, and next action

Customers need to see the finished shape. These examples show what could be delivered as a PDF, webpage, Google Sheet, checklist, or planning packet.

Input

What the customer sends

Photos, notes, screenshots, measurements, customer details, links, old quotes, or rough ideas.

Review

What gets cleaned up

Missing info, risks, confusing pieces, decisions, categories, and next steps.

Output

What they receive

Snapshot, tracker, webpage, quote sheet, project packet, form, or workflow plan.

Next Step

What they do next

Send missing info, approve scope, quote the job, build, organize, launch, or follow up.

Project 1: Garage / Shop Layout Cleanup Packet

Sample customer: Iron Range Garage Help has a 28' x 40' garage, two overhead doors, scattered tools, storage problems, and wants a usable plan before buying shelves or moving equipment.

Customer Sends

  • Photos from each corner
  • Rough outside dimensions
  • Door locations and vehicle needs
  • Toolbox, bench, compressor, welder, and storage goals
  • Notes about what is always in the way

Northwoods Output

  • Garage zones: vehicle, workbench, storage, tool, walkway
  • Missing measurements checklist
  • Phase plan before buying materials
  • Safety/clearance notes
  • Basic material shortlist
Deliverable AOne-page layout summary
Deliverable BMeasurement checklist
Deliverable CPhase-by-phase action plan
Deliverable DStarter material list

Boundary: This is a planning packet, not stamped drawings, engineering, electrical design, or permit-ready construction work.

Project 4: Yard / Sprinkler Planning Packet

Sample customer: Lakeside Yard Demo wants a rough irrigation concept before buying parts or asking a contractor for a quote.

Customer Sends

  • Satellite screenshot
  • Yard photos from each side
  • Water source location
  • Driveway, trees, garden beds, fence
  • Watering goals and rough budget

Northwoods Output

  • Watering area map notes
  • Possible zone list
  • Head placement concept
  • Trench path concerns
  • Questions for pressure/flow and local rules
AreaGoalPlanning NoteVerify Before Buying
Front lawnEven coverageSplit driveway side from main lawnWater pressure and flow
Garden bedsGentle wateringConsider drip or separate zonePlant water needs
Back yardWide coverageWatch trees and fence lineUtility locate and trench path
Boundary: This is layout planning only. Pressure/flow checks, backflow rules, utility marking, permits, and installation must be verified by the owner or proper professional.
