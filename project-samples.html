Input

What the customer sends

Photos, notes, screenshots, measurements, customer details, old quotes, product links, or rough ideas.

Cleanup

What gets sorted

Missing info, risk points, categories, decisions, next steps, and deliverable structure.

Output

What they receive

Snapshot, tracker, webpage, quote sheet, planning packet, checklist, or workflow map.

Next Action

What they do next

Measure, send photos, approve scope, quote the job, organize files, launch, or follow up.

Project Example 1: Garage / Shop Layout Cleanup Packet

Sample customer: Iron Range Garage Help has a 28' x 40' garage, two overhead doors, scattered tools, storage problems, and wants a usable plan before buying shelves or moving equipment.

Customer Sends

  • Photos from each corner
  • Rough outside dimensions
  • Door locations and vehicle needs
  • Workbench, toolboxes, compressor, welder, and storage goals
  • Notes about what is always in the way

Northwoods Output

  • Garage zones: vehicle, workbench, storage, tool, walkway
  • Missing measurements checklist
  • Phase plan before buying materials
  • Safety and clearance notes
  • Starter material shortlist

Project Example 4: Yard / Sprinkler Planning Packet

Sample customer: Lakeside Yard Demo wants a rough irrigation concept before buying parts or asking a contractor for a quote.

Customer Sends

  • Satellite screenshot
  • Yard photos from each side
  • Water source location
  • Driveway, trees, garden beds, fence
  • Watering goals and rough budget

Northwoods Output

  • Watering area map notes
  • Possible zone list
  • Head placement concept
  • Trench path concerns
  • Questions for pressure/flow and local rules
AreaGoalPlanning NoteVerify Before Buying
Front lawnEven coverageSplit driveway side from main lawnWater pressure and flow
Garden bedsGentle wateringConsider drip or separate zonePlant water needs
Back yardWide coverageWatch trees and fence lineUtility locate and trench path
Boundary: Layout planning only. Pressure/flow checks, backflow rules, utility marking, permits, and installation must be verified by the owner or proper professional.
